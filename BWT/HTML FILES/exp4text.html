Festivals of India

India is a country of dynamic culture, and every Indian celebrates many festivals wholeheartedly throughout the year. Every festival is unique in its way. Celebrating them while you pay a visit to the country is a great chance to explore the prosperity of Indian culture and heritage.

This is a link to Festivals of India, a website that provides a month-wise list of Indian festivals and their dates, significance, and attractions.

This is a table of some of the popular festivals of India:

Name Date Significance
Diwali October-November The festival of lights, celebrating the victory of good over evil
Christmas December 25 The festival of the birth of Jesus Christ
Eid al-Fitr May-June The festival of breaking the fast, marking the end of Ramadan
Holi February-March The festival of colours, celebrating the arrival of spring and the triumph of love
Guru Nanak Jayanti November The festival of the birth of Guru Nanak, the founder of Sikhism
